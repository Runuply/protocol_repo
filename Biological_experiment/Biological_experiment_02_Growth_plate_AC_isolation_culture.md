# Mouse Growth Plate Chondrocyte Isolation and Primary Culture

## Purpose

This protocol describes isolation and primary culture of mouse growth plate chondrocytes from neonatal mouse knee joints.

## Materials

- Neonatal mice, typically P5-P7
- Sterile PBS, preferably without calcium and magnesium
- Protease from *Streptomyces griseus* / pronase, 1 mg/ml in PBS
- Collagenase type II, 1 mg/ml in alpha-MEM containing 10% FBS
- Complete alpha-MEM culture medium
- 12-well plate
- 15 ml conical tubes
- Sterile forceps, scissors, and dissection tools
- Stereomicroscope
- CO2 incubator at 37°C
- Optional: 70 um cell strainer

## Reagent Notes

- Protease stock: 3 mg/ml, stored at -20°C.
- Collagenase type II stock: 10 mg/ml, stored at -20°C.
- Prepare working enzyme solutions fresh or thaw immediately before use.
- Use sterile technique for all tissue handling and cell culture steps.

## Procedure

1. Euthanize neonatal mice according to the approved animal protocol.

2. Dissect knee joints and isolate growth plate cartilage tissue, typically from the distal femur and proximal tibia.

3. Under a stereomicroscope, carefully remove attached ligaments, tendons, muscle, and other non-cartilage tissues.

4. Place the roughly cleaned growth plate cartilage pieces into a well of a sterile 12-well plate.

5. Wash the tissue twice with sterile PBS.

6. Add 500 ul of protease from *Streptomyces griseus* / pronase working solution at 1 mg/ml in PBS, enough to cover the cartilage tissue.

7. Incubate at 37°C for 1 hour.

8. Transfer the cartilage tissue together with the protease solution into a 15 ml conical tube.

9. Add 4 ml PBS and gently pipette up and down to loosen attached soft tissue and debris. Discard the loosened non-cartilage material while retaining the cartilage pieces.

10. Repeat step 9 until no more small soft-tissue fragments detach from the growth plate cartilage.

11. Transfer the cleaned growth plate cartilage pieces into a fresh well of a sterile 12-well plate.

12. Add 500 ul of collagenase type II working solution at 1 mg/ml in alpha-MEM containing 10% FBS, enough to cover the cartilage tissue.

13. Tilt the 12-well plate so the tissue remains submerged in the collagenase solution, and incubate in a 37°C CO2 incubator overnight, approximately 12 hours.

14. Add 1 ml complete alpha-MEM or PBS to the well, then gently pipette up and down to dissociate the digested cartilage into a single-cell suspension.

15. Transfer the growth plate chondrocyte suspension into a 15 ml conical tube. Add PBS or complete alpha-MEM to wash/dilute the suspension to approximately 5 ml total volume.

16. Optional but recommended: filter the suspension through a 70 um cell strainer to remove undigested tissue fragments.

17. Centrifuge at 500 x g for 5 minutes at room temperature.

18. Carefully discard the supernatant.

19. Resuspend the cell pellet in an appropriate volume of complete alpha-MEM.

20. Count the cells and assess viability.

21. Seed growth plate chondrocytes into suitable culture plates or dishes according to the downstream experiment.

## Expected Yield

- Example yield: both knees from 2 P7 mice can yield approximately 6 x 10^6 chondrocytes.
